# Live Demo Script — Peter

So this is the BestPath dashboard.

We are going to run our data analysis here — this process examines patient information, references a huge library of clinical standards, and surfaces patients sorted into three categories: needing action now, needing action soon, and on track.

Now, for a given patient, let's say Stephanie Clark, we can click to view details.

From this screen, you can see the full analysis path, based on this patient's visits, and cross-check the findings directly against the source documents. The system explains why an action is recommended, and why it should be done now.

Stephanie is long-overdue for a blood test, which is key for diabetic care and monitoring.

Once the clinician has read the suggestions and approved them, they can send email and text-message notifications to the patient in a single click. The system automatically writes messages, and they can be customized further as needed.

Activity is tracked, and patients can be marked as completed once a clinician has reviewed their information.

All of this is designed to get patients early, preventative care before they end up in emergency.

Now, from the patient side, BestPath uses the same powerful analysis engine to direct people to an appropriate care provider.

Let's say you're someone without a family doctor. You come to BestPath, and it asks you a few simple questions — your age, sex, any conditions you're managing, medications you're on. It's conversational, not a checklist.

Behind the scenes, it searches the same clinical guidelines database, and then gives you a set of prioritized recommendations — colour-coded by urgency. Each one tells you not just what to do, but who to see and what to say when you get there.

So instead of wondering whether you should go to a walk-in, a pharmacist, or a lab, you get a clear, specific next step — grounded in the same evidence a clinician would use.

And importantly, this is not medical advice. It's guidance toward the right type of care provider, at the right time.
